Monochromatic Card

Monochromatic color palettes use different shades and tints of one color. They are harmonious and can be accented with a complementary color for emphasis. However, it's a good idea to add a complementary color to a monochromatic palette in web design in order to draw attention to important elements.

Three-Color Scheme Card

Adjacent color palettes consist of three evenly spaced colors on the color wheel. These palettes are ideal for playful or artistic designs.

Complementary Color Card

Complementary palettes use colors opposite on the color wheel to create balance and visual tension. One color serves as the primary, the other as an accent.

Exercise 2: Basics of Colors (Level-1)

I learned that a monochromatic color scheme uses various shades and tints of a single color. This creates a smooth, harmonious design, but to avoid it looking too plain, I can add accents or textures to give it more depth and interest.

I also learned that a complementary color scheme uses colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel, like red and green or blue and orange. This contrast makes the colors pop, but I need to be careful not to overuse them, as too much contrast can be harsh. Balancing these colors with neutrals helps to make the design more pleasant.

Lastly, I learned about the three-color scheme, which uses three colors spaced evenly on the color wheel. This creates a balanced, dynamic look while still feeling cohesive. It's great for adding accent colors and highlighting specific elements, providing variety without overwhelming the design.
