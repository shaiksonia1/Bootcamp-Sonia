The Beauty of CSS Design

To create a typographic hierarchy on my page, I combined multiple font families to distinguish between different content types, such as headers and body text. For the headings, I used Playfair Display, a serif font, which adds elegance and a strong visual presence to titles and subheadings. I adjusted the font weight, letter-spacing, and line-height to make the headers stand out without compromising readability. For the body text, I selected Roboto, a clean sans-serif font, known for its legibility. I tweaked the font size, line height, and letter-spacing to ensure the body text is comfortable to read on various screen sizes, contributing to a balanced and aesthetically pleasing layout.

In addition to these Google Fonts, I also used a locally stored font, Geist, for key emphasis areas on the page. This added a unique touch while maintaining readability. By adjusting font properties like size, weight, and spacing, I ensured that the content hierarchy was clear and visually appealing. The careful combination of these fonts allows for a seamless flow of information, with headers catching attention while the body text remains easy to read, improving both the usability and the design of the page.

So What is This About?

To implement a typographic hierarchy, I used multiple font families for headers and body text, adjusting font sizes, weights, and spacing for better readability. For the headers, I used Playfair Display with a larger font size and increased letter-spacing to give it a bold and elegant appearance. I set the line-height to 1.2 to ensure the text has enough vertical space, making it more visually appealing and easier to distinguish from the body text.
